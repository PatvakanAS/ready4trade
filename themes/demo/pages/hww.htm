{{ 'Our approach'|_ }}

{{ 'Ready4Trade Central Asia is guided by the following principles:'|_ }}

  • {{ 'Customized activities tailored to each beneficiary country`s specific needs.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'An inclusive approach to its work focusing on women`s economic empowerment.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'Sustainable long-term results produced through capacity-building activities with multiplying effects.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'Synergies fostered among all relevant stakeholders, including beneficiaries from the private and public sector, as well as other developments agencies.'|_ }}
{{ 'Objectives'|_ }}
{{ 'Establishing Trade Facilitation Portals'|_ }}

{{ 'In order to ensure the beneficiary countries’ compliance with the WTO Trade Facilitation Agreement, enhance the transparency and predictability of cross-border trade formalities and identify potential areas for the simplification of procedures, Ready4Trade Central Asia will:'|_ }}

  • {{ 'Create, launch and promote National Trade Facilitation Portals in four Central Asian countries – Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan. In Tajikistan, the “Tajikistan Trade Portal” was developed as part of the Global Textiles and Clothing Programme (GTEX) implemented by ITC and funded by the State Secretariat for Economic Affairs SECO. Now, Ready4Trade is expanding and updating the “Tajikistan Trade Portal”.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'Train local stakeholders to run the portal in a sustainable manner.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'Map formalities for a minimum of 20 import & export flows.'|_ }}

{{ 'Addressing obstacles to trade'|_ }}

{{ 'To remove obstacles on both sides of the borders for an increased intra-regional and extra-regional trade, Ready4Trade Central Asia will:'|_ }}

  • {{ 'Identify the most stringent barriers to trade and pinpoint their root causes.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'Draft relevant reforms to address these barriers with the support of both the public and private sectors.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'Support the implementation of reforms in compliance with existing regional and international agreements.'|_ }}

{{ 'In Turkmenistan, the project does not work on addressing obstacles to trade. Instead, it is providing initial technical assistance on WTO accession-related issues.'|_ }}

{% partial 'svg/animtochki' %}
{{ 'Helping SMEs comply with cross-border requirements'|_ }}

{{ 'To enable more SMEs to participate in regional and international value chains and generate new export flows, Ready4Trade Central Asia will:'|_ }}

  • {{ 'Develop a cross-border management curriculum tailored to Central Asian business needs comprising of 20 modules.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'Support local institutions in delivering this curriculum through e-learning platforms and instructor-led workshops.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'Provide on-the-job coaching at the premises of selected SMEs in partnership with local institutions in each beneficiary country as part of an “Export Management Coaching Initiative”.'|_ }}
{% partial 'svg/animtochki' %}
{{ 'Supporting women in trade'|_ }}

{{ 'To raise awareness of border officials on gender-specific challenges and boost women entrepreneurs` confidence and their capacity to engage in international trade, Ready4Trade Central Asia will:'|_ }}

  • {{ 'Conduct research and develop technical reports on the specific challenges that women entrepreneurs face and gather data on gender issues, including on border officials’ gender perception.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'Develop and deliver training addressing gender and trade issues for border officials, statistics agencies and women entrepreneurs.'|_ }}
{% partial 'svg/animtochki' %}
{{ 'Supporting SMEs’ engagement in e-commerce'|_ }}

{{ 'To raise SMEs` capacity to trade digitally, both intra-regionally and internationally, Ready4Trade Central Asia will:'|_ }}

  • {{ 'Assist 200+ Central Asian businesses in online by providing individual coaching, conducting customized research for SMEs and working with private sector partners to offer technical help.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'Help national Business Support Organizations better understand the needs of SMEs on their e-commerce journey.'|_ }}
{% partial 'svg/animtochki' %}
{{ 'Developing E-commerce Strategies and e-commerce legislation'|_ }}

{{ 'To enable beneficiary countries to develop a strategic vision and strengthen the e-commerce competitiveness of SMEs, Ready4Trade Central Asia will:'|_ }}

  • {{ 'Support governments in Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan and Uzbekistan in developing national e-commerce strategies.'|_ }}
  • {{ 'Assist in the development of an e-commerce regulatory framework in Kazakhstan (in cooperation with UNCTAD).'|_ }}
